About Cornicing

A cornice is a decorative finish used in homes to neatly finish off the join between the wall and ceiling. It can be attached with nails, however with new technology and the use of polystyrene cornices, the process of installing the finish has become a lot faster and can now easily be glued into place.

Installing a polystyrene cornice is simple with the right adhesive. Clean the wall and ceiling surfaces, apply our cornice adhesive along both the wall and ceiling contact edges of the cornice, press firmly into position and hold for a few seconds. Mitre the corners for a professional finish. Wipe away excess adhesive immediately.

For best results, use Elegant Cornicing adhesive, specifically formulated for our cornice range.

Our Traditional Range comes in 2.5 metre lengths, while our Moulded Range comes in 2 metre lengths. Both ranges offer a wide variety of profiles to suit different aesthetic preferences and budgets.

About Polystyrene

Polystyrene (PS) is a synthetic aromatic polymer made from the monomer styrene. It is a hard, quite brittle plastic, typically used in CD cases, disposable razors, plastic cutlery, and similar items.

Expanded Polystyrene (EPS) is this same brittle plastic that has been foamed using a gas, generally Butane or Pentane. EPS is typically used in insulation for cold rooms, packing material, disposable plates and cups, and — in our case — cornicing.

EPS is created in a two-stage process:

  1. Raw beads are expanded using steam, creating pre-puff beads, which are then cured in large bags before being conveyed to the mould.
  2. Once in the mould, the pre-puff beads are again steamed to form a block measuring 2550mm × 1250mm × 960mm.

The blocks are then placed on the storage floor to age for the appropriate length of time before being cut into various shapes and sizes. Blocks are produced in several densities to meet specific project needs.

Sustainability & Recycling

Elegant Cornicing is very proud to be a green manufacturer with our very own in-house recycling machine.

Did you know? Less than 10% of plastic manufactured worldwide is recycled, as opposed to more than 90% of steel. Weight for weight, plastic is actually more expensive than steel, making it a very valuable commodity that is completely overlooked.

Simply put — we only have one Earth and one chance to look after it. Recycling is very important as waste has a huge negative impact on the natural environment. Plastic has an estimated lifespan in a landfill of more than 450 years. Harmful chemicals and greenhouse gases are released from rubbish in landfill sites.

Recycling helps to reduce the pollution caused by waste and also reduces the need for raw materials so that the environment can be preserved.

It really is a simple process:

  1. Collect EPS that can be recycled from manufacturing waste.
  2. Crush/chip the EPS and store in large storage bins (our bins stand 6m high and hold a massive volume).
  3. Transfer material to a 2-process extruding and pelletising line, which produces reusable pellets.
